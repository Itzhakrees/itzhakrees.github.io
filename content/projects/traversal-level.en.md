# Traversal Level Case Study

## Overview

This greybox study explores route readability, pacing contrast, and encounter staging inside a compact traversal space.

## Design Ownership

- Built a route plan with a visible goal, two optional detours, and a controlled return path.
- Used sightlines and elevation changes to preview upcoming decisions.
- Staged encounters around movement confidence instead of pure damage pressure.

## Key Design Questions

- Can the player understand the primary route without relying on UI markers?
- Do optional paths feel like meaningful discoveries rather than dead ends?
- Does the level alternate compression and release at a readable pace?

## Outcome

The strongest result was the return path. Reusing a familiar space from a different angle made the level feel more connected while keeping production scope small.
